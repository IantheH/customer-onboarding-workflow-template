# Customer Kickoff Call Agenda

A kickoff call sets the tone for the customer relationship. The goal is to confirm business objectives, align stakeholders, clarify expectations, and define the first path to value.

---

## 1. Welcome & Introductions

**Goal:** Establish rapport and confirm everyone’s role.

Discussion points:

- Who is attending from the customer team?
- Who is the primary point of contact?
- Who are the decision-makers, admins, and daily users?
- What role does each stakeholder play in adoption?

---

## 2. Business Goals

**Goal:** Understand what the customer wants to accomplish.

Questions:

- What business problem are you trying to solve?
- Why did you choose this platform?
- What does success look like in the first 30, 60, and 90 days?
- What outcomes matter most to your team?
- Are there any deadlines, launch dates, or business priorities we should know about?

---

## 3. Current Workflow Review

**Goal:** Understand how the customer works today.

Questions:

- What tools are you currently using?
- What manual processes are slowing the team down?
- Where do handoffs happen?
- What information needs to be tracked?
- What workflows should be improved first?

---

## 4. Platform Setup Needs

**Goal:** Identify what needs to be configured before adoption can begin.

Areas to review:

- User access
- Roles and permissions
- CRM fields or pipelines
- Templates
- Automations
- Integrations
- Dashboards or reporting needs
- Notification settings

---

## 5. Training & Enablement

**Goal:** Define how users will learn the platform.

Questions:

- Who needs training?
- What workflows should training focus on?
- Do users prefer live training, recorded walkthroughs, written documentation, or a mix?
- What questions or concerns do users already have?

---

## 6. Risks & Blockers

**Goal:** Identify issues that could slow adoption.

Questions:

- Are there any internal blockers?
- Are there competing priorities?
- Is there missing data, access, or documentation?
- Are stakeholders aligned on the goals?
- Are there any technical limitations or dependencies?

---

## 7. First Value Milestone

**Goal:** Define the first meaningful win.

Examples:

- First workflow configured
- First automation launched
- First customer record created
- First dashboard reviewed
- First campaign launched
- First integration connected
- First team member trained

[Add milestone here]

---

## 8. Next Steps

**Goal:** End with clear ownership and momentum.

Action items:

Action Item | Owner | Due Date | Status 

---

## Kickoff Call Success Criteria

The kickoff call is successful when:

- Business goals are clear
- Primary stakeholders are identified
- Initial workflows are understood
- Setup needs are documented
- Risks and blockers are captured
- First-value milestone is defined
- Next steps and owners are confirmed
