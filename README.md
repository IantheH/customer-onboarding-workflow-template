# Customer Onboarding Workflow Template

A practical customer onboarding framework for SaaS teams focused on helping customers move from kickoff to confident product adoption.

This template is designed around the customer success lifecycle: understanding goals, mapping workflows, guiding setup, supporting adoption, and creating clear next steps.

---

## Purpose

Customer onboarding is not just setup. It is the first stage of long-term customer success.

A strong onboarding process should help customers:

- clarify their business goals
- understand how the platform supports those goals
- identify key stakeholders and users
- define first-value milestones
- reduce confusion during setup
- build confidence using the product

---

## Onboarding Stages

### 1. Discovery & Goals

Understand what the customer is trying to accomplish.

Key questions:

- What problem are you trying to solve?
- What does success look like in the first 30, 60, and 90 days?
- Who are the primary stakeholders and users?
- What workflows need to be supported?
- What risks or blockers should we know about?

---

### 2. Workflow Mapping

Document how the customer works today and where the SaaS platform fits.

Areas to map:

- current tools
- manual processes
- customer journey steps
- handoffs between teams
- reporting needs
- automation opportunities

---

### 3. Setup & Configuration

Configure the platform around the customer’s goals and workflows.

Examples:

- user access
- CRM fields or pipelines
- automations
- integrations
- templates
- dashboards
- notification settings

---

### 4. Training & Enablement

Help users understand how to use the platform with confidence.

Training should cover:

- core workflows
- daily usage habits
- common questions
- support paths
- documentation
- next steps after onboarding

---

### 5. First Value Milestone

Define the first meaningful outcome the customer should reach.

Examples:

- first campaign launched
- first workflow automated
- first dashboard reviewed
- first customer record created
- first integration connected
- first team member trained

---

### 6. Adoption Check-In

Follow up after initial setup to confirm usage, answer questions, and identify blockers.

Check-in questions:

- What is working well?
- Where are users getting stuck?
- Are the original goals still accurate?
- What feature or workflow should we focus on next?
- What documentation or training would help?

---

## 30 / 60 / 90 Day Customer Success Plan

### First 30 Days

Focus: setup, clarity, and first value.

- Complete kickoff
- Confirm goals
- Map workflows
- Configure core setup
- Train primary users
- Achieve first-value milestone

### First 60 Days

Focus: adoption and consistency.

- Review usage patterns
- Resolve blockers
- Improve workflows
- Add automations or integrations
- Support additional users
- Document repeatable processes

### First 90 Days

Focus: business outcomes and long-term value.

- Review goals and progress
- Identify adoption gaps
- Recommend next workflows or features
- Align on success metrics
- Confirm ongoing support rhythm
- Prepare for account review or QBR

---

## Customer Health Signals

Positive signals:

- active usage
- stakeholder engagement
- workflow completion
- support responsiveness
- adoption of core features
- clear business goals

Risk signals:

- low usage
- missed meetings
- unclear ownership
- repeated support issues
- unresolved blockers
- lack of executive or stakeholder engagement
